Section 1 — Metals

Stainless Steel

Defined by a minimum 10.5% chromium content that forms a self-healing passive oxide layer. The four main families — austenitic (300 series), martensitic/ferritic (400 series), precipitation hardening (PH), and duplex — each target distinctly different applications and buyer profiles.

🔎 SEO Connection

Industrial buyers searching for stainless steel almost always include the grade. "316L stainless plate", "17-4PH bar stock", and "2205 duplex tube" are high-intent, purchase-ready keywords. Build a dedicated page for each grade a client stocks or processes — generic "stainless steel" category pages serve as hubs, but grade-level pages are where conversions happen.

Section 2 — Metals

Carbon Steel, Alloy Steel & Tool Steels

The backbone of industrial manufacturing. Carbon steels are defined by carbon content — low, medium, and high. Alloy steels add chromium, molybdenum, or nickel for enhanced strength and hardenability. Tool steels are engineered specifically for cutting, forming, and die work.

💡 Keyword Insight

Carbon steel searches are almost always specification-driven: "A36 steel plate cut to size", "4140 round bar annealed", "H13 tool steel block". ASTM designations (A36, A572, A514) dominate structural keywords. SAE/AISI numbers (1018, 4140, 8620) dominate machining and bar stock searches. Tool steel grades are often searched with condition (annealed, hardened) as a qualifier — build pages that include the condition.

Section 3 — Metals

Aluminum Alloys

Aluminum alloys are organized into series by primary alloying element. The designation system includes a 4-digit series number plus temper designation (e.g., -T6, -O, -H32). Temper is critical to understanding the material's actual strength and formability — always include it in content and keyword targeting.

🔎 SEO Connection

Aluminum searches include both the series and the temper: "6061-T6 aluminum bar", "7075-T651 aluminum plate", "5052-H32 aluminum sheet". The temper is not optional from an SEO standpoint — it's a differentiator that signals exactly which buyer you're targeting. A page titled "6061 Aluminum" is weaker than "6061-T6 Aluminum Bar Stock" for converting a procurement buyer comparing vendors.

Section 4 — Metals

Copper & Copper Alloys

Copper alloys encompass pure copper, brasses (copper-zinc), bronzes (copper-tin and others), and specialty alloys like beryllium copper. They are distinguished by thermal and electrical conductivity, corrosion resistance, antimicrobial properties, and bearing characteristics. UNS designations (C110, C360, etc.) are the standard reference system.

🔎 SEO Connection

Copper alloy buyers search by UNS designation, common name, and application. "C360 free machining brass bar", "C110 copper sheet", "C932 bearing bronze bushing", and "beryllium copper strip" are all high-intent searches. Note that buyers often mix common names and UNS numbers in searches — build content that bridges both. A page for "Naval Brass (C464)" captures both search formats.

Section 5 — Metals

Exotic & Specialty Alloys

Exotic alloys are specified when standard steels and aluminum fall short — primarily in demanding environments involving extreme heat, severe corrosion, radiation, or ultra-high strength-to-weight requirements. They command premium pricing and are procured through specialized distributors. Buyers searching for these materials are almost always engineers or procurement specialists with an active project need.

🔎 SEO Connection

These are the highest-value, lowest-volume keywords in the industrial search landscape. "Inconel 718 bar stock", "Ti-6Al-4V plate DFARS compliant", and "Hastelloy C-276 sheet" represent buyers with large-budget, time-sensitive procurement needs. If a client stocks or distributes these materials, these pages should be the first content investments — not afterthoughts. The intent-to-value ratio is exceptional.

Section 6 — Plastics

Engineering Plastics

Engineering plastics replace metals in applications requiring corrosion resistance, electrical insulation, weight reduction, or self-lubrication. Buyers typically search by trade name (Delrin, Teflon, UHMW) as well as generic name (acetal, PTFE, polyethylene). Industrial plastic suppliers stock these in standard plate, rod, and tube forms that are then machined to specification.

🔎 SEO Connection

Plastic material searches blend trade names and generic names in equal measure. Build content that bridges both: "Delrin (Acetal) Rod", "UHMW Polyethylene Sheet", "Teflon (PTFE) Bar Stock". Industrial buyers searching for machinable plastics frequently include the form (rod, sheet, plate, tube) and sometimes the size or color (white, black, natural). FDA-compliant and USP Class VI plastics have very specific compliance-driven search terms that convert at extremely high rates.

Section 7 — Plastics

High-Temperature Plastics

High-performance thermoplastics and thermosets capable of continuous service above 300°F (150°C). These materials are specified in demanding environments where standard engineering plastics fail — particularly aerospace, semiconductor, chemical processing, and medical sterilization. They carry significant price premiums over standard engineering plastics and are almost exclusively used in precision-machined applications.

🔎 SEO Connection

Buyers searching for high-performance plastics are highly specialized — "PEEK rod for autoclave", "Torlon bar stock", "Vespel bushing", and "Ultem sheet FDA" are all examples of high-intent, expert-buyer searches. Trade names dominate over generic chemical names in search. If a client machines or distributes these materials, the investment in specific grade + form pages pays back disproportionately relative to the content effort required.

Temperature Rating Context

Temperature ratings listed are continuous service temperatures — the maximum for long-term structural use. Short-term exposure limits are typically 15–25% higher. Always clarify with clients whether their application is continuous-duty or intermittent, as this changes the appropriate material selection. For content writing: note that buyers often search by application environment ("plastic for autoclave", "LOX-compatible seal material") rather than by specific grade.

Section 8 — Reference

Common Forms & Shapes

Industrial buyers search by material and form. Understanding the difference between a plate and a sheet, a seamless pipe and a welded tube, or a hot-rolled bar and a cold-drawn bar is essential for writing accurate content. These distinctions are not interchangeable — using the wrong term signals to a technical buyer that the content isn't credible.

📐
Plate
Steel · Stainless · Aluminum · Copper
Typically ≥ 3/16" thick

Flat product in heavier gauges. Used for structural, pressure vessel, and fabricated component applications. Ordered by thickness × width × length.

📄
Sheet
Steel · Stainless · Aluminum · Copper
Typically < 3/16" thick

Flat product in lighter gauges. Used for stamping, forming, enclosures, and architectural panels. May be cut-to-size or supplied in coils.

🔄
Coil / Strip
Steel · Stainless · Aluminum · Copper
Continuous roll form

High-volume continuous sheet product supplied in roll form. Used for roll forming, stamping presses, and slitting operations.

⚫
Round Bar / Rod
All Metals · Plastics
OD specified in inches or mm

Most common bar form. Foundation of turned/machined parts — shafts, pins, fasteners. Available hot-rolled (HR) or cold-drawn (CD); CD offers tighter tolerances.

⬛
Flat Bar
Steel · Stainless · Aluminum
Width × Thickness specified

Rectangular cross-section bar. Used for brackets, keys, weld fixtures, and structural members. Distinct from plate in that it is supplied in bar lengths.

🔷
Hex Bar
Steel · Stainless · Aluminum · Brass
Across-flats dimension

Hexagonal cross-section. Preferred for fasteners, valve stems, and fittings that require wrench-flat surfaces. Across-flats (AF) dimension is the key spec.

⬜
Square Bar
Steel · Stainless · Aluminum
Side dimension specified

Square cross-section bar stock. Used for keys, tool holders, structural members, and machined components requiring a square profile.

⭕
Round Tube
Steel · Stainless · Aluminum · Copper
OD × Wall or OD × ID

Hollow cylindrical product. Structural tube (HREW, DOM) vs. mechanical tube — specify the end use. Also available as hydraulic tubing with tighter tolerances.

🟦
Square / Rect. Tube
Steel · Stainless · Aluminum
OD × OD × Wall

Hollow square or rectangular section. Widely used in structural fabrication, frames, equipment guards, and architectural applications (HSS — hollow structural section).

🔵
Pipe (Seamless & Welded)
Steel · Stainless · Aluminum
Nominal Pipe Size (NPS) + Schedule

Dimensioned by nominal pipe size and schedule (wall thickness). Seamless for pressure applications; welded for general use. Specify ASTM grade, schedule, and end finish.

📐
Angle (L-Shape)
Steel · Stainless · Aluminum
Leg × Leg × Thickness

L-shaped structural section. Available equal and unequal leg. Ubiquitous in structural fabrication, frames, brackets, and supports. Steel angles are among the highest-volume structural shapes.

🔲
Channel (C / U-Shape)
Steel · Stainless · Aluminum
Depth × Width × Web thickness

C-shaped structural section. Used for frames, structural tracks, conveyor supports, and machine bases. Standard channel (C) vs. MC channel (taper-flange) — distinguish in content.

🏗️
Wide Flange Beam (W-Shape)
Steel
Depth × Weight per foot

I-shaped structural beam with near-parallel flanges. The primary structural member in steel construction. Specified by nominal depth and weight per linear foot (e.g., W8×31).

〰️
Extrusion (Custom & Standard)
Aluminum (primary) · Copper
Custom or standard cross-section

Aluminum extrusions can achieve nearly any consistent cross-section. Standard shapes (angles, channels, T-slots) are stocked; custom shapes require tooling. 6063 is the extrusion workhorse alloy.

🔩
Forgings
Steel · Stainless · Aluminum · Ti · Ni Alloys
Near-net-shape or rough forging

Compressive forming at elevated temperature. Produces superior grain structure, strength, and reliability vs. castings. Open-die (large/custom shapes) or closed-die (high-volume, near-net-shape).

🪛
Wire & Welding Products
Steel · Stainless · Aluminum · Copper · Ni Alloys
Diameter in inches or AWG

Wire for springs, fasteners, fencing, and electrical. Welding wire/rod (MIG, TIG, stick) is a distinct product line — specified by AWS classification, diameter, and packaging.

Form-Specific Keywords

The form is a critical keyword modifier. "4140 steel round bar", "6061 aluminum plate", and "316L stainless tube" are different pages with different buyers. A buyer searching for bar stock is not the same buyer searching for plate — their application, order quantity, and processing needs differ. Always build pages at the material-grade-form intersection when the client stocks that specific combination.

Section 9 — Reference

Common Machining Services

Understanding machining processes is essential for writing accurate capability pages and service descriptions. Buyers searching for a machine shop almost always specify the process, material, and often a certification. Each service below represents a distinct page opportunity for a machining client — and a distinct set of buyer intent signals to target.

🔄
CNC Turning / Lathe
Rotating workpiece machined by a stationary cutting tool. Produces cylindrical, conical, and threaded features. Multi-axis turning centers handle complex geometries in one setup.
All MetalsEngineering PlasticsPEEKNylon
⚙️
CNC Milling (3, 4 & 5-Axis)
Rotating cutting tool removes material from a stationary (or indexed) workpiece. Produces prismatic parts with pockets, slots, profiles, and complex 3D contours. 5-axis allows complex geometries in one setup.
All MetalsCompositesPlastics
⚡
Wire EDM
Electrical discharge machining using a thin wire electrode. Cuts hardened tool steels and exotic alloys with extreme precision. No cutting forces — ideal for fragile or thin cross-sections. Tolerances to ±0.0001".
All Conductive MetalsTool SteelsCarbide
🔷
Sinker / Ram EDM
Shaped electrode (graphite or copper) sinks into workpiece to create cavity. Used for blind pockets, rib details, and complex die cavities that cannot be milled conventionally.
Tool SteelsHardened MetalsCarbide
🔲
Surface Grinding
Abrasive wheel removes material from flat surfaces to achieve very tight thickness tolerances and excellent surface finish (Ra). Commonly used for mold plates, gage blocks, and precision fixtures.
SteelStainlessTool SteelsHardened Parts
⭕
Cylindrical Grinding
External (OD) or internal (ID) grinding of cylindrical features to achieve extremely tight diameter tolerances and fine surface finish. Used for shafts, bores, and mating precision fits.
ShaftsHardened PartsTool Steels
💧
Waterjet Cutting
High-pressure water + abrasive garnet cuts virtually any material with no heat-affected zone (HAZ). Cuts stacks of material simultaneously. Excellent for heat-sensitive alloys, composites, and thick sections.
All MetalsCompositesPlasticsGlassStone
🔆
Laser Cutting
High-powered laser beam cuts material with narrow kerf and high repeatability. Fiber laser for metals; CO₂ for non-metals. Excellent for thin-to-medium sheet. Fastest process for complex 2D profiles.
Sheet SteelStainlessAluminumCopper
🔥
Plasma Cutting
Ionized gas arc cuts electrically conductive metals at high speed. Well-suited for thicker plate where laser cutting becomes economically unfavorable. Wider kerf than laser; some HAZ.
Structural SteelStainlessAluminumHeavy Plate
🔩
Threading & Tapping
Internal (tapping) and external (threading/thread milling) thread production. Specified by thread standard (UN, ISO Metric, ACME, NPT/NPTF), class of fit, and material hardness. Critical for fluid system fittings.
All MetalsPlastics
🌡️
Heat Treatment
Modifies mechanical properties through controlled heating and cooling. Common processes: annealing (soften), normalizing, quench & temper (harden), stress relieve, carburizing/nitriding (case harden), solution anneal + age harden (PH alloys).
Carbon SteelAlloy SteelTool SteelStainless PHTi Alloys
✨
Surface Finishing
Post-machining finishes that enhance corrosion resistance, appearance, hardness, or dimensional accuracy. Key processes: anodizing (Al), passivation (SS), electropolishing (SS), black oxide (steel), hard chrome, nickel plating, powder coat.
Aluminum (Anodize)Stainless (Passivate)Steel (Plate/Coat)
✂️
Sawing / Cut-to-Length
Precision sawing of bar, tube, and structural shapes to customer-specified lengths. Band saw, cold saw, and circular saw each offer different combinations of speed, finish, and kerf. High-volume cut-to-length is a major service offering for metals service centers.
All MetalsPlastics
🔨
Forming & Bending
Press brake bending, roll forming, and hydroforming change the geometry of flat or tubular stock without removing material. Tolerances depend on material springback properties — an important variable in aluminum and HSLA steels.
Sheet SteelStainlessAluminumCopper
🔗
Welding & Fabrication
Joining processes including TIG (GTAW), MIG (GMAW), stick (SMAW), and resistance welding. AWS D1.1 (structural steel) and ASME Section IX (pressure vessel) are the most commonly cited weld qualifications in industrial procurement.
SteelStainlessAluminumExotic Alloys

Inspection & Certification
CMM (coordinate measuring machine) dimensional inspection, material certifications (MTRs / mill certs), hydrostatic testing, NDT (ultrasonic, x-ray, dye penetrant, magnetic particle). Certifications like AS9100, ISO 9001, and ITAR are page-level SEO topics.
