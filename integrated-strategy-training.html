02 — Integrated Fluency

Every Teammate
Speaks Strategy

You don't need to execute across all channels to understand them. Integrated fluency isn't about doing everything — it's about knowing how your work connects to everything else. Channel specialization lives in execution. Strategic understanding belongs to everyone.

01
Know Who You're Serving
Every team member should be able to articulate the client's ICP, what they sell, who buys it, and why. B-SMART® is the shared vocabulary — use it regardless of your channel.
02
Know What's Active & Why
Understand what each active channel is doing for this client and how it ladders to the broader business goal — even the channels you don't personally execute in.
03
Know the Current Story
Be ready to speak to what's working, what's at risk, and what's coming next. The performance story is shared property — not siloed by channel.

🎯 The 6 Account Questions

For any active account, every person on the team should be able to answer these — not from memory, but from staying engaged with the work.

1
Buyer Intelligence
Who is this client's buyer and what does their search journey look like?
What are they searching, when, why, and with what intent? An engineer requesting a quote searches very differently than an ops manager checking availability. This context shapes every channel's strategy.
2
Channel Awareness
What channels are active on this account, and what role is each one playing?
Not just "we run SEO and Paid" — but what is SEO building right now, what is Paid targeting, and how are they covering different parts of the funnel together?
3
Performance Story
What's the current performance status — where are we winning, and where are the gaps?
"Organic is climbing in positions but conversions are lagging. Paid is converting well but pacing tight." That's the level of fluency expected from everyone on the account.
4
Strategic Direction
What are we prioritizing this period and what's the reasoning behind it?
Priorities should have a "why" the whole team understands. If a channel is focusing on bottom-funnel keywords this quarter, everyone should know the business reason behind that decision.
5
Cross-Channel Impact
How does your channel's work affect the other active channels on this account?
A landing page rewrite affects Paid Quality Scores. A content calendar affects Paid's ability to front-run launches. A new negative keyword list informs what SEO shouldn't be building toward. Know the ripple.
6
The Integrated Brief
How would you explain our integrated approach to this client in 60 seconds?
Not a feature list — a coherent story about how the channels work together to drive the outcome the client cares about. This is the most important test of integrated fluency.

How to Speak Integrated

When a client asks about a channel you don't directly execute in, you have two options. One builds trust. One erodes it.

🚫 Don't Say This

"That's more of a Paid Search question — you'd want to talk to whoever handles that side."

✅ Say This Instead

"Great question — our SEO and Paid strategies are intentionally aligned here. What we're building organically around X is designed to complement the terms we're bidding on. Want me to walk you through how they connect?"

🚫 Don't Say This

"I'm not sure what the Paid team is doing with the budget right now — I'd have to check."

✅ Say This Instead

"We're pacing well on Paid this month — the focus has been on high-intent [material/product] terms that convert fastest. On the SEO side, we're building organic content that will eventually reduce reliance on that spend."

⚠️
No One Arrives Cold
Every person attending a kickoff, strategy call, or client check-in must review the website, analytics, active channel performance, and recent communications before joining. Showing up without context isn't neutral — it signals to the client that their account isn't a priority.

04 — Strategy Development & Execution

The Strategy Loop

Strategy isn't a document you write at kickoff and revisit at renewal. It's a continuous loop — built from real data, refined through collaboration, and expressed through every tactical decision made on behalf of the client. Here's how it works at OBx.

🔄
Strategy vs. Execution — Know the Difference
Execution is completing a task. Strategy is knowing why that task matters and how it connects to the client's goals. Every team member should be able to speak to both layers of the work — not just what got done, but what it was designed to accomplish and what we're learning from it.

🗂️ The Account Development Arc

Every account moves through a predictable development arc. Strategy conversations at each phase look different — but the integrated mindset stays consistent.

🏗️
Month 1
Foundation & Discovery
Keyword research, keyword mapping, technical audit, analytics setup, homepage optimizations, and strategy creation. The conversations here center on understanding the client's ICP, competitive landscape, and establishing performance baselines.
⚡
Month 2–3
Activation & Optimization
Base optimizations for pages ranking positions 4–20, new landing pages from gap analysis, content development, full page optimizations, and top/bottom-of-page content. Strategy conversations shift to what the data is showing and how to push striking-distance opportunities.
📈
Quarterly+
Evolution & Expansion
Every 90 days, strategy is reviewed and rebuilt for the next quarter. Wins are scaled, gaps are addressed, new opportunities are surfaced. The conversation expands: what's the next phase of growth, and where should cross-channel energy be focused?

05 — Shared Vocabulary

The B-SMART® Method

B-SMART® is OBx's proprietary keyword framework — and the shared language of every channel on every account. When SEO, Paid, UX, and Email all use the same taxonomy, strategy compounds instead of collides. If someone on the team doesn't know B-SMART®, they can't speak integrated.

🏭
Why B-SMART® Matters for Everyone
Industrial and B2B buyers search with specificity and intent — material grades, certifications, tolerances, dimensions, geographies. B-SMART® maps every dimension of how these buyers find what they need. If you can describe a client's product through this lens, you can contribute meaningfully to their strategy — regardless of which channel you execute in.

🗺️ The Three Search Layers — 2026

Search is no longer one thing. Every strategy must account for all three layers — and the team should be able to speak to where a client lives in each of them.

01
Traditional Organic
Blue Links — Still Matters, Different Signals
The 10 blue links haven't disappeared — but their composition has changed. Keyword-only strategies are dying. What remains is topical authority, genuine E-E-A-T signals, and content that serves real users rather than algorithms.
  • Keyword rankings still matter — but intent fit matters more
  • Technical health is table stakes — not a differentiator
  • Topical authority and depth beat volume
  • User behavior signals (CTR, engagement) heavily weighted
02
AI Search (AIO / AI Mode)
Google's AI Layer — In the SERP Itself
AI Overviews appear above organic results for informational queries. AI Mode is a full reimagining — users ask 2–3× longer questions and explore conversationally. Both are now active in 200+ countries. Ads appear in both placements.
  • AIO eats top-of-funnel informational clicks — the Great Decoupling
  • Being cited in AIO = a new form of visibility worth optimizing for
  • AI Mode rewards depth, authority, and structured content
  • Ads above/below AIO and within AI Mode are live and expanding
03
LLM / AI Assistants
External AI Tools — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini
Users are researching in AI chatbots before they ever open a browser. 40% of AI Mode users say they're using ChatGPT less — but this is still a real and growing discovery channel that requires a brand presence strategy.
  • LLM citations favor authoritative, structured, well-sourced content
  • Brand mentions across Reddit, YouTube, and industry publications inform LLM outputs
  • IndexNow helps content get indexed faster — and picked up by AI tools sooner
  • Traffic from LLMs tracks as Referral/Unassigned in GA4 — must be custom segmented

🔍 GEO & AEO — What These Actually Mean

Two terms every team member should be able to define and apply — not as buzzwords, but as strategic lenses that inform how content is built and what constitutes visibility in 2026.

🤖
GEO — Generative Engine Optimization
Optimizing content to be cited in AI-generated answers
  • →
    E-E-A-T as the Citation Filter
    AI systems — both Google's and external LLMs — surface content from brands that demonstrate Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trust. Content built on first-hand experience and original insight gets cited. Generic content gets ignored. 49.6% of SEO professionals are already investing here.
  • →
    Brand Presence Beyond Google
    LLMs learn who your brand is from third-party mentions — Reddit threads, YouTube, industry publications, trade press. A client with no presence in these channels is invisible to AI systems. Building genuine brand presence outside of owned properties is now a GEO strategy, not just a PR play.
  • →
    Content AI Cannot Replicate
    Original data, first-hand walkthroughs, credible author bios, process documentation with real photos, expert opinions — these are the content types AI will cite rather than generate itself. This is the content our clients should be building. This is what the SEO strategy should prioritize in 2026.
  • →
    IndexNow as a GEO Tool
    Quick indexing via Bing Webmaster Tools and IndexNow doesn't just help traditional search — it improves how content is picked up by AI tools like ChatGPT that rely on up-to-date indexed content. This is a standard recommendation on all accounts.
💬
AEO — Answer Engine Optimization
Structuring content to directly answer high-value questions
  • →
    AI Mode Rewards Depth and Structure
    AI Mode users ask 2–3× longer queries. They're researching, comparing, evaluating — not just looking for a quick answer. Content needs to be structured to satisfy multi-part, complex questions that traditional 300-word pages can't handle. Depth beats breadth in this environment.
  • →
    Featured Snippets & People Also Ask
    Structured content that directly answers common questions — in clear, well-organized formats — wins Featured Snippets and PAA placements. These are also the content structures most frequently surfaced in AI Overviews. AEO and traditional on-page SEO reinforce each other.
  • →
    Schema Markup as Machine-Readable Context
    Structured data tells both Google's AI and external LLMs exactly what a piece of content is about, who created it, and why it should be trusted. Schema markup is no longer optional for clients competing for AI visibility — it's foundational infrastructure.
  • →
    TOFU Is Not Dead — It's Shifted
    Top-of-funnel informational content is losing organic click value as AI Overviews answer those queries in-SERP. The strategy shift: build TOFU content that earns AI citations (GEO), use it for brand awareness, and let bottom-funnel transactional content carry conversion weight in organic and paid.

💰 AI in Paid Search — What's Changed and What's Available

The paid search landscape has been restructured around AI. Understanding these formats — even at a high level — is necessary for every team member to speak intelligently about a client's paid strategy.

⚡
AI Max for Search Campaigns
Google's next evolution of Smart Bidding + Targeting
  • +
    27% More Conversions
    Campaigns using AI Max see 27% more conversions at a similar CPA/ROAS compared to campaigns running primarily on exact and phrase match keywords. The signal: keywordless targeting unlocks searches advertisers weren't reaching before.
  • +
    Three Core Features
    Search Term Matching — captures demand based on intent, not just exact queries. Final URL Expansion — sends traffic to the most relevant page for the user's query. Automatically Created Assets — generates ad copy relevant to the specific search, improving relevance at scale.
  • +
    Controls Are Still Available
    AI Max is not a black box. Negative keywords, brand inclusions/exclusions, text guidelines, URL inclusions/exclusions, and location targeting at ad group level are all available. The goal is to guide the AI — not override it at every turn. The search term report surfaces "AI Max" as the source column for keywordless matches.
📍
Ads in AI Overviews & AI Mode
New ad placements inside Google's AI search experiences
  • →
    Ads in AI Overviews — Live in the US
    Shopping Ads and Text Ads now appear above and below AI Overview responses, plus within the AIO itself. Launched in all 200+ countries where AI Overviews are available. Eligible campaign types: Search, Shopping, Performance Max, App, Local.
  • →
    Ads in AI Mode — Active Experiment
    Shopping Ads and Text Ads are now appearing below the first AI Mode response — an active experiment in the US for desktop and mobile. AI Mode users ask significantly longer, more complex queries. Ads that appear here are answering deep research-phase questions — a higher-funnel opportunity than traditional paid.
  • →

📊 Measuring AI Traffic in GA4 — What the Team Needs to Know

AIO / AI Mode
Google's AI Search Features
Clicks from AI Overviews and AI Mode are classified as Organic Search in GA4. Google treats these as a natural extension of the search journey — a link from Google to your site, just in a new format.
There is no way to segment AIO/AI Mode clicks from standard organic clicks in the current default view. To measure impact, monitor overall Organic Search performance for changes in traffic volume, engagement rate, and conversions.
⚠️ Cannot segment from organic — monitor holistically
AI Assistants
ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Copilot
Traffic from external AI tools lands in the Referral or Unassigned default channel in GA4. This can be segmented by creating a Custom Channel Group with an "AI Assistants" channel.
Use this regex as the matching condition under Session Source:
^.*ai|.*\.openai.*|.*chatgpt.*|.*gemini.*|.*gpt.*|.*copilot.*|.*perplexity.*|.*gemini.*google.*$
✓ Custom channel group available now — native channel coming soon
🔮
What's Coming in GA4
Google is building a native "AI Assistants" channel in the Default Channel Group that will automatically categorize traffic from top external AI tools — removing the need for manual custom setup. Until then, the custom channel group approach above is the recommended setup for all accounts where AI referral tracking is relevant.

Happy to walk through what it would look like for your account.

07 — Standards

Non-Negotiable Standards

These aren't suggestions — they're the operating principles that protect the integrity of the work, the trust of the client, and the compounding effectiveness of every channel running under the OBx banner.

01
One Unified Client Voice
The client should never hear conflicting information from different channels. All strategic POVs are aligned internally before any external communication goes out. Channels contribute — the team sends one response. The client hears one story.
02
Strategy Before Tactics
Never execute on a tactic that hasn't been tied to a strategic goal. Every keyword, every ad, every piece of content should trace back to a client objective. Busy work is not billable value — and it can't be explained in a strategy conversation.
03
Data Informs Everything
Opinions without data are guesses. Every strategic recommendation should be grounded in SERP analysis, search term data, GA4 performance, or competitive intelligence. Bring receipts to every meeting — internal or client-facing.
04
Respect the Sales Cycle
B2B clients have 90-day+ sales cycles. Don't evaluate channel performance through a 30-day lens when leads take months to close. Judge the inputs — quality traffic, qualified leads, improving engagement — before making revenue calls too early.
05
Intent Is the Compass
Every keyword decision — in SEO and Paid — starts with intent classification. Informational terms shape content strategy. Transactional terms drive conversion-focused pages and ads. In an AI-first world, this distinction matters more than ever — not less.
06
Adapt to the Landscape
The search environment described in this playbook will change again. AI search formats are evolving rapidly, measurement is imperfect, and new ad placements are launching monthly. The standard isn't to have all the answers — it's to stay current, stay curious, and update the strategy when the landscape shifts.
