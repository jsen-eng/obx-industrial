Section 1 — The Foundation

We Generate Opportunities, Not Just Metrics

There's a fundamental difference between reporting on what happened and generating what matters. Every team member — regardless of channel specialty or account role — is responsible for connecting their work to pipeline-relevant outcomes for the client.

❌ The Metric Reporter
Stops at the number
  • "Traffic is up 22% this month."
  • "We generated 47 leads from paid search."
  • "Bounce rate improved by 8%."
  • "Organic rankings improved for 12 keywords."
  • "CTR is up on Brand campaigns."
  • "We sent 3 email campaigns with a 24% open rate."
  • Reports activity — but leaves the client to connect it to their business
✅ The Opportunity Generator
Connects every metric to pipeline impact
  • "Traffic grew 22% — 8 of those sessions led to spec-complete RFQ submissions from aerospace procurement leads."
  • "47 paid leads — 12 were quote-ready based on Loop scores; 3 moved to proposal stage."
  • "Lower bounce rate is because buyers are now landing on the grade-specific page they were searching for."
  • "The 12 ranking improvements are driving discovery from engineers searching for DFARS-compliant titanium."
  • "Email re-engaged 4 leads from last quarter's form submissions — 2 submitted new RFQs."
  • Reports outcomes — and recommends next actions based on what was generated
The Core Principle

The shift isn't just about framing — it's about how you build strategy. When you think in opportunities, you make different keyword decisions, different content decisions, different budget decisions. You ask different questions. You bring more value. This playbook is about operationalizing that mindset across every channel and every role at OBx — regardless of vertical.

Why This Matters to Clients

  • Clients don't buy traffic — they buy opportunities to grow their business
  • Metric reports create dependency; opportunity reports build strategic partnership
  • Clients who understand what's being generated invest more — and stay longer
  • When marketing and sales align around quality, the client's close rate improves alongside our performance
  • It's the difference between a vendor relationship and a Heartbeat-healthy account

Why It Matters to Your Work

  • Opportunity thinking surfaces better keyword targets — buyers with intent, not just volume
  • It prioritizes the right content investments — pages that convert, not just rank
  • It sharpens PPC decisions — budget follows opportunity quality, not click quantity
  • Email sequences become purposeful — re-engaging the right leads, not the full list
  • It makes your strategy defensible because it's grounded in real buyer data
Section 2 — The Channel Roles

Four Channels, Each With a Specific Job

SEO, Paid Search, Email, and UX are not interchangeable — each has a distinct role in generating and advancing opportunities. Understanding how they differ is what allows them to work together. Loop is the data layer that continuously sharpens all four using real buyer signals.

🔍
SEO / Organic Search
Top-of-Funnel Discovery Engine
Primary Job
Attract the right buyers at the right moment of active intent — before they've decided who they want to work with.
  • Matches buyer search language to client capabilities using the B-SMART® framework
  • Builds authority for specific materials, certifications, applications, and industries
  • Generates discovery opportunities — buyers in research mode who find you instead of a competitor
  • Creates compounding assets: content that generates opportunities for months and years
  • Earns visibility across the full buying journey — early research through vendor shortlisting
Connects to Loop: ICP language and industry patterns from Loop form data feed keyword strategy and content prioritization
💰
Paid Search
Demand Capture + Acceleration Engine
Primary Job
Capture high-intent, in-market demand and accelerate time-to-opportunity — especially for buyers with urgency or specificity.
  • Targets buyers with active, specific intent: material + grade + quantity + application using B-SMART®
  • Puts the client in front of buyers who are ready to RFQ or request a quote now
  • Tests messaging and offers quickly — validating what converts before scaling organically
  • Budget is attached to every click — opportunity quality determines whether spend is justified
  • Accelerates the buyer journey for prospects with immediate need or compressed timelines
Connects to Loop: ICP role, industry, and language patterns tell you which keywords to bid, which to block, and how to write ad copy
📧
Email
Pipeline Velocity + Re-Engagement Engine
Primary Job
Nurture leads that aren't ready to buy yet, re-activate past opportunities, and move contacts from interested to ready without new acquisition spend.
  • Keeps the client top-of-mind with buyers in long consideration cycles
  • Re-engages leads that submitted forms but haven't converted to quote or sale
  • Segments based on what buyers submitted — material, urgency, role, industry — for targeted nurture
  • Delivers value (specs, certifications, case studies) to accelerate buyer confidence
  • Converts "not yet" leads into active pipeline — recovering opportunities that go quiet
Connects to Loop: ICP segmentation from form content drives who gets which sequence, with what language, at what cadence
🖥️
UX / Web Experience
Conversion Confidence Engine
Primary Job
Remove friction so buyers who've already decided to engage can take action without hesitation — making every other channel more efficient.
  • Ensures landing pages match the intent of the ad or organic result that brought the buyer
  • Eliminates content gaps — buyers shouldn't need to call to find specs, certs, or lead times
  • Optimizes forms to collect qualification data without adding unnecessary barriers
  • Reduces repeated page visits before converting — a signal of missing information, not buyer indecision
  • Builds buyer confidence through trust signals: certifications, response time, in-stock messaging
Connects to Loop: Behavioral friction patterns and repeated page visit data from Loop tell you exactly where to fix and what to add

Section 4 — ICP Intelligence

Building Your Client's ICP From Loop Data

The most accurate picture of who a client's ideal customer actually is doesn't come from a kickoff call — it comes from who actually submits. Every form fill is a data point. Patterns across dozens, then hundreds, of real submissions become a living ICP: who's buying, what they ask for, how they describe it, and which channels bring the best ones in. That ICP sharpens every channel decision you make.

❌ The Assumed ICP
"Who we think is buying"
  • Built from a kickoff call and the client's own assumptions — often reflects who they want to serve, not who's actually showing up
  • Rarely updated — becomes stale within months as market conditions and buyer mix shift
  • Uses internal product naming and client terminology, not how buyers actually search and describe their needs
  • No volume or frequency data — no way to separate a real pattern from a single anecdote
  • Produces campaigns and content built around presumed behavior — expensive when the assumptions are wrong
  • Can't identify which channels bring the highest-fit buyers vs. noise — so budget decisions are guesses
✅ The Loop-Revealed ICP
"Who is actually buying — backed by submission data"
  • Built from real form submissions: company, role, industry, location, request type, and language used by every buyer who contacted you
  • Updates every month — gets sharper as volume accumulates and reflects actual shifts in buyer mix over time
  • Captures the exact language buyers use — the shorthand, grades, specs, and application terms they actually type and say
  • Volume and frequency patterns emerge: which requests repeat, which companies resubmit, what urgency patterns appear
  • Produces campaigns and content grounded in observed, real demand — not assumptions from a discovery session
  • Identifies which channels bring ICP-aligned buyers vs. low-fit volume — enabling defensible budget and content decisions
Why ICP Clarity Is the Foundation of All Channel Strategy

A clear ICP doesn't just help the client — it changes how you work across every channel. It tells SEO which pages to build and what language to use. It tells Paid which keywords to bid and which to block. It tells Email who to re-engage and how to speak to them. It tells UX where buyers are getting stuck and why. Without Loop data shaping your ICP, channel decisions are assumptions. With it, they're evidence. The ICP is not a document you build once at kickoff — it's a living profile that Loop updates every single month.

Request Language & Terminology
How buyers describe what they need — your best keyword data
What Loop Captures
  • Exact material names, grades, and buyer shorthand from comment fields: "HR coil," "DOM tube," "2205 plate," not what the client calls it internally
  • Spec language: dimensions, tolerances, finishes, certifications mentioned (DFARS, MTR, ISO, AMS) — buyer vocabulary, not marketing vocabulary
  • Urgency language: "ASAP," "end of month," "Q3 project," "planning for next year" — intent depth signals
  • Application language: "for a pressure vessel," "aerospace contract," "FDA-regulated process" — use case context that informs content targeting
  • Qualification questions in comments: "do you have minimum orders?", "what are lead times?" — content gap signals hiding in plain sight
"Is the language on our pages and in our ads what buyers actually use — or are we creating friction between how they search and what they find when they land?"
📦
Volume, Quantity & Frequency
Order patterns that reveal buyer type and procurement depth
What Loop Captures
  • Requested quantity — spot buyers (small one-offs) vs. volume procurement buyers (recurring POs, blanket orders)
  • Submission frequency — one-time contacts vs. companies that submit monthly or quarterly signal different relationship potential
  • Request variety — same company asking for different materials or products signals a broader procurement relationship worth pursuing
  • Seasonal patterns — certain materials, industries, or applications cluster around specific months or quarters, enabling proactive campaign timing
  • Low-volume outliers — repeated small-quantity requests are a messaging and UX problem: wrong buyers are finding you and you're not filtering them
"Are we generating procurement-level buyers or spot buyers — and are our messaging and UX doing enough to filter the volume that doesn't fit the client's minimum thresholds?"

Industry & Sector Patterns
Which verticals are finding you — and which should be
What Loop Captures
  • Industry or sector from the form field and company domain: defense, aerospace, automotive, construction, medical, energy, food processing
  • Industries over-indexing vs. your target mix — a vertical appearing without deliberate effort is an inbound demand signal worth validating
  • Compliance requirements by industry: DFARS for defense, ITAR for export, FDA for medical, ISO for aerospace — reveals what compliance content gaps exist
  • Industry-specific language and spec patterns — aerospace buyers write differently than HVAC contractors; content and ads should reflect that
  • Industries submitting but converting poorly signal a UX, content, or fundamental ICP mismatch worth diagnosing before investing more budget
"Which industries are generating the best opportunities — and are there industries submitting that we haven't built dedicated pages, campaigns, or sequences for yet?"

Geographic & Location Patterns
Where buyers are — and what it means for reach and investment
What Loop Captures

  • Regional industry clusters: defense contracts in certain states, automotive in the Midwest, energy in the Gulf Coast — informs targeting strategy
  • Shipping inquiry patterns in comments ("do you ship to [region]?") signals an underserved territory with active demand
  • Geographic spread vs. client assumptions — many clients are surprised by how widely they're actually reaching without deliberate geo expansion
  • High submission volume + low conversion by region signals a shipping coverage, lead time, or competitive gap specific to that territory
"Are our paid and organic targeting settings aligned with where our best buyers actually are — or are we defaulting to a geographic assumption that was never validated by real submission data?"

ICP Pattern Recognition: If You See This, Do This

These are the patterns that surface consistently when you read Loop submissions with intention. Each one is a specific signal tied to a specific channel action — recognize them early and act before a competitor does.

Language Gap Buyers consistently use different terminology in comments than what appears on the site — "HR coil" vs. "hot rolled coil," "DOM tubing" vs. "drawn over mandrel"
This gap costs you CTR, ranking relevance, and form conversion simultaneously — because the buyer's mental model doesn't match what they find when they land. Update product page copy, meta titles, ad headlines, form labels, and email subject lines to use buyer terminology. A single language alignment pass across all four channels can improve performance across all of them at once.
💰 Paid 🔍 SEO 🖥️ UX 📧 Email
Unplanned Industry Multiple submissions from an industry sector the client wasn't actively targeting — appearing in Loop data without any deliberate effort to reach them
This is inbound demand that already exists. Validate with the client: can they serve this industry well? If yes, this is a straightforward opportunity — build a dedicated landing page, launch an industry-specific campaign, create a nurture sequence, and add the vertical's compliance and application language to the SEO pipeline. You're capturing demand you didn't have to generate.
💰 Paid 🔍 SEO 📧 Email
Content Gap The same questions appear repeatedly in comment fields — "do you carry X grade?", "what are your lead times?", "are you DFARS compliant?" — questions not answered anywhere on the site
Every repeated comment question is a direct content brief. Buyers are contacting you to find information they couldn't find themselves — that's friction you created. Build the page, add the FAQ, update the product page inline, and add the topic to PPC ad copy as a trust differentiator. The volume of repetition tells you exactly how much that gap is costing you in lost conversions.
🔍 SEO 🖥️ UX 💰 Paid
Role Mismatch High-scoring submissions consistently come from a role your content isn't built for — engineers submitting but content is written for owners, or procurement managers converting best but pages are full of technical spec language
Content that doesn't speak to the role generating the best opportunities creates unnecessary friction at the most important moment. Identify the highest-converting role combination in Loop, then audit whether your landing pages, ad copy, email sequences, and form CTAs speak to their primary concerns. Build at least one dedicated experience for that role — and update everything else to at least acknowledge them.
🔍 SEO 💰 Paid 📧 Email 🖥️ UX
Wrong-Fit Pattern Low-scoring submissions cluster around specific sources, keywords, or buyer language — retail buyers, hobbyists, or single-unit requests that aren't worth the client's sales time
Wrong-fit leads aren't a volume problem — they're a targeting and messaging problem. Add minimum order callouts to the most affected pages. Extract wrong-fit language from submissions and add it as negative keywords. Update ad copy to qualify buyers before the click: "Industrial quantities · 50 lb minimum." Brief the client: showing them you're actively filtering bad fits from their sales queue is a Heartbeat-building conversation.
💰 Paid 🔍 SEO 🖥️ UX
Repeat Submitter The same company has submitted 2–4 times across different months with no record of converting — they keep returning but haven't closed
Repeat submitters without conversion are a sales process signal, not just a marketing failure. Flag these companies to the client immediately — is sales following up? Is there a pricing issue, lead time concern, or certification gap stalling the deal? Simultaneously: make the return experience better with a more direct contact pathway, an FAQ addressing common hesitations, and an email re-engagement sequence targeted at this specific pattern.

Section 7 — Reporting & Ownership

Lead With What Was Generated — Every Role Owns This

Opportunity-first reporting changes the client relationship. Instead of reviewing what happened, you're analyzing what was generated, what the ICP data revealed, and what you're doing differently next month. That's a strategic partnership — and it's not just the account lead's job.

The Three Questions Every Report Should Answer

1
How many relevant opportunities did we generate this period — and from which channels?
Don't lead with sessions or impressions. Lead with qualified inbound activity: spec-complete form submissions from ICP-matching company types and roles, quote-intent calls, email re-engagements that generated new submissions. Break it down by channel so the client can see where investment is creating pipeline. Use Loop's company, role, and industry data to distinguish volume from quality — and bring both to the conversation.
2
What did the ICP data this month tell us about our buyer mix, language, and channel quality?
Every month of Loop data is a month of ICP refinement. Who submitted — what company types, what roles, what industries? What language appeared in comment fields that should change our copy or keywords? Were there new patterns in volume, geography, or industry that should shift our targeting? Bring 2–3 specific Loop ICP insights into every client conversation — this is what makes the client trust you're operating on real market intelligence, not a stale kickoff persona.
3
What are we changing because of it — and what signal will we watch for next period?
The report shouldn't end at data. It should end at action. Based on what the ICP data and opportunity review revealed, what are you adjusting in SEO, Paid, Email, or UX — and why? What specific signal will you track to know if it worked? This closes the loop for the client and proves that the intelligence you're gathering actively shapes their strategy. That's a strategic partner. That's a Heartbeat score that stays above 85.

Every Role Owns This — Not Just the Account Lead

The Universal Principle

Role assignments vary by account. Strategic principles are universal. Every team member should be able to articulate the integrated strategy — not just their tactical lane. Whether you own keywords, campaigns, sequences, or page layouts, your output feeds the same ICP and the same pipeline outcome. Know how your work connects to what gets generated.

🔍 SEO Specialist

Content, keywords, and organic authority

  • Uses Loop ICP language data to build keyword targets from real buyer terminology — not keyword planner estimates alone
  • Tracks which landing pages appear in the highest-quality, ICP-matched conversion paths using Loop behavioral data
  • Tailors content depth to the roles appearing most in high-scoring submissions — engineers get spec tables, owners get outcomes
  • Builds certification and compliance hub pages around the requirements ICP buyers ask about most frequently in comments
  • Reports which new ranking improvements drive ICP-matched sessions and form fills — not just traffic lift

💰 PPC Manager

Campaigns, bids, and demand capture

  • Uses Loop ICP language data to add exact-match keywords from buyer comment fields — bypassing keyword planners entirely for the most specific terms
  • Extracts wrong-fit submission language from Loop ICP to build negative keyword lists that filter wrong company types and buyer profiles
  • Adjusts bids based on which keywords drive ICP-matching submissions, not just total click volume
  • Reports campaign quality by ICP alignment: which campaigns drove the right company types, roles, and industries this month
  • Mirrors ICP buyer language in ad headlines — if the ICP comment data says "in stock" and "ASAP," those are headline tests waiting to run

📧 Email Coordinator

Nurture, re-engagement, and pipeline velocity

  • Segments sequences based on Loop ICP dimensions: material requested, industry, role, urgency signals, and time since submission
  • Builds re-engagement flows from submitted-but-dormant leads segmented by ICP profile — different industries, roles, and request types get different sequences
  • Uses buyer language from Loop comment fields in subject lines — if procurement managers at aerospace companies respond to "DFARS-compliant," that's the subject line
  • Tracks re-engagement to return form submission as the primary success metric — not open rate alone
  • Feeds re-engagement signals back to the team: which ICP segments are coming back, and what does it tell us about their buying timeline?

🖥️ UX / Developer

Conversion experience and friction reduction

  • Uses Loop behavioral path data to identify exactly where ICP buyers are getting stuck or repeating before converting
  • Prioritizes form field additions based on what ICP buyers consistently ask in comments that current fields don't capture
  • Adds trust signals (certs, response commitments, in-stock callouts) specifically where Loop shows the ICP's most common drop-off points
  • Flags pages that appear frequently in ICP-buyer conversion paths as high-value expansion priorities for SEO
  • Measures success in terms of ICP-matched conversion rate improvement — not just aesthetic improvement or page speed in isolation

📋 Account Manager

Client strategy, relationship, and communication

  • Brings 2–3 specific Loop ICP insights to every client conversation — patterns and data, not opinions and assumptions
  • Connects reporting to ICP quality and opportunity alignment — then asks the client to validate against what their sales team is seeing
  • Uses ICP data to ask sharper strategy questions: "We're seeing a lot of aerospace submissions — what does your team's capacity look like for that vertical?"
  • Facilitates the marketing-to-sales feedback loop: does the ICP Loop is building match who the client's team actually wants to close?
  • Maintains Heartbeat health through regular ICP-led conversations, proactive strategy pivots, and timely reports grounded in real buyer data
